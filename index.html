Maxwell's Equations Visualized

Divergence measures spreading out.

Before Maxwell, meet the dot: divergence asks whether a field flows outward from a point, inward toward it, or simply passes around without piling up.

Operator meaning

∇ · F

Positive divergence means field flows outward like a source. Negative divergence means field flows inward like a drain. Zero divergence means no net source or sink.

What you are seeing

The arrows are a vector field. When more arrows leave the transparent surface than enter it, divergence is positive. When more enter than leave, divergence is negative.
